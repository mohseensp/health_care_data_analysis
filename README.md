# health_care_data_analysis
This project focuses on analyzing healthcare data to uncover meaningful insights related to patient demographics, disease patterns, hospital performance, and treatment costs. The dataset includes information about patients, medical conditions, hospitals, doctors, billing amounts, admission types, and duration of hospital stays.

The analysis aims to identify key trends such as the most common medical conditions, distribution of patients across age and gender, and the relationship between hospitals and revenue generation. It also evaluates hospital efficiency by comparing patient volume, average billing per patient, and yearly revenue performance.

Additionally, the project explores doctor workload distribution, identifies specialists handling critical conditions like cancer, and examines operational factors such as admission types and insurance coverage. A key highlight of the analysis is the identification of critical medical conditions based on longer hospital stays, higher treatment costs, and emergency admissions.

The insights derived from this project help in understanding healthcare system dynamics, optimizing resource allocation, improving patient care strategies, and supporting data-driven decision-making in hospital management.
